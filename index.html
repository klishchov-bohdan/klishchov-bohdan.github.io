Features

Ultimate gameplay

In the definition process, you can choose backstory elements that will influence the ultimate gameplay.

Character

You can customize your character's abilities, and the process for customizing appearance is extremely detailed, including body type, skin tone, tattoos, makeup, and more.

Aspects of your character

The aspects of your character's appearance will influence the way that other people interact with you.

Body modification

In Cyberpunk 2077, getting body modifications seems to be more than just picking upgrades from a list.

Backpack

The backpack provides an overview of all the different items currently in your inventory.

Minimap

The minimap could use more zoom levels so that you can see upcoming turns better while driving.

Main characters

Judy Alvarez

Techie

Judy Alvarez is a skilled braindance technician and member of the Moxes in Cyberpunk 2077.

Jackie Welles

Solo

Jackie Welles is a character in the video game Cyberpunk 2077. He is one of V's early points of contact as well as the player's partner during several missions. He is Spanish-speaking and has no love for Scavengers.

Johnny Silverhand

Rockerboy

Johnny Silverhand is a central character throughout the Cyberpunk series, as well as an influential Rockerboy and the lead singer of the band Samurai before the breakup in 2008

Viktor Vector

Ripperdoc

Viktor Vector, or Vik for short, is a ripperdoc in Cyberpunk 2077. His operating room is located behind Misty's shop. Talented, patient, and professional, Viktor has extended V's credit on a number of occasions, but never complains.

Yorinobu Arasaka

Rockerboy, CEO

Yorinobu Arasaka is the second son to Saburo Arasaka, and rebellious bosozoku leader of the Steel Dragons as well as close affiliate of Iron Lotus. Although a sworn bosozoku leader Rockerboy, he eventually became the heir to the Arasaka Corporation Empire. He is the main antagonist of Cyberpunk 2077.

Goro Takemura

Bodyguard

Goro Takemura is Saburo Arasaka's personal bodyguard in Cyberpunk 2077.
